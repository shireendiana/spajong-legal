1. What information do we collect?

Personal information you provide to us

In short: we collect the personal information you give us when you create an account or contact us.

  • Account information. When you create an account you provide an email address and a password, and you choose a display name. Sign-in is handled by our authentication provider, Supabase. We never see or store your password — it is managed securely by Supabase. Your email is used to identify your account and to contact you about the App.
  • Profile information. Your display name.
  • Messages you send us. If you use the App’s contact feature, your message opens in your device’s email app and is sent to us by email; we receive whatever you choose to include.

Sensitive information. We do not process sensitive personal information.

Information created as you use the App

  • Settings. Your preferences — tile theme, card year, whether sound and haptics are enabled, and potentially others.
  • Gameplay history. For each game — the outcome, difficulty level, card year, duration, points, and the winning hand pattern. This powers your play history and practice progress.
  • Subscription and trial status. When you create an account, a free-trial period is started; we record its status and start/end dates so the App knows whether you have access to paid features. During the beta, no payment information is collected and no payment is required.

Information collected automatically

In short: some device and usage information is collected automatically when you use the App. It does not reveal your identity but helps us keep the App secure and understand how it’s used.

  • Usage and analytics data. We use Google Analytics for Firebase to understand how the App is used — which screens are viewed, which tile themes and bot voices are used, and how games are configured and completed. This data is pseudonymous: it is tied to a random account identifier (a UUID), not to your name or email. We have not enabled advertising or data-sharing features in our analytics.
  • Approximate location. Our analytics provider derives an approximate, city-level location from your device’s IP address at the time data is collected. The IP address is used only to estimate this location and is then discarded — it is not stored by us or our provider. We use this only to understand, in aggregate, where our players are. We do not collect precise or GPS-based location.
  • Device information. Our analytics and crash-reporting providers collect technical details such as device model, operating system version, screen characteristics, and app version, to understand which devices our players use and to diagnose problems.
  • Crash and diagnostic data. We use Firebase Crashlytics to record crashes and errors so we can fix them. This includes standard technical information (device model, OS version, app version, crash details). Crash reports may be associated with the same pseudonymous identifier described above.

Information we do not collect

We do not collect your real name (beyond a display name you choose), home address, or phone number. We do not collect precise or GPS-based location. We do not use advertising SDKs, and we do not build advertising profiles. We do not collect information about you from third parties.

Push notifications

We may request to send you push notifications about your account or features of the App. If we do, you can opt out at any time in your device’s settings.

2. How do we process your information?

In short: we process your information to provide and improve the App, communicate with you, maintain security, and comply with law.

We use the information described above to:

  • create and manage your account and keep you signed in across devices;
  • save your settings and gameplay history so your experience carries over between sessions;
  • determine whether you have access to free-trial or paid features;
  • diagnose crashes and errors and keep the App stable and secure;
  • understand how the App is used, in aggregate and pseudonymous form, so we can improve it;
  • understand, in aggregate, the approximate locations and device types of our players;
  • send you administrative messages (such as account information and changes to our terms or policies);
  • respond to messages you send us.

We do not use your information for marketing or advertising, and we do not sell or rent it.

3. What legal bases do we rely on?

In short: we only process your information when we have a valid legal reason to do so.

If you are located in the EU or UK, the GDPR and UK GDPR require us to explain the legal bases we rely on. We may rely on:

  • Consent — where you have given us permission for a specific purpose. You can withdraw consent at any time.
  • Performance of a contract — to provide the App and the services you request.
  • Legitimate interests — to operate, secure, and improve the App, including diagnosing problems and understanding usage, where those interests are not overridden by your rights. We do not rely on legitimate interests to send marketing.
  • Legal obligations — to comply with applicable law, or to exercise or defend legal claims.
  • Vital interests — in rare cases, to protect someone’s safety.

If you are located in Canada, we process your information with your consent (express or implied), which you can withdraw at any time, and in the limited circumstances permitted by applicable Canadian law.

4. When and with whom do we share your information?

In short: we share information only with the service providers that operate the App on our behalf, and in limited legal situations.

We use a small number of trusted service providers. They process data on our behalf, under their own security and privacy commitments:

  • Supabase — provides account authentication and stores your account, profile, settings, gameplay history, and trial status in a database. Access is row-level protected, so each user can only read and write their own data.
  • Firebase (Google) — provides pseudonymous usage analytics (Google Analytics for Firebase) and crash reporting (Firebase Crashlytics).
  • Apple — distributes the App (including via TestFlight during the beta) and processes related information under Apple’s own privacy policy.

We may also share or disclose information:

  • For legal reasons — where required by law, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of Spajong or its users.
  • Business transfers — in connection with a merger, financing, acquisition, or sale of all or part of our business.

We do not share your information with any other third parties.

5. Do we use cookies or other tracking technologies?

In short: we use analytics and crash reporting only. We do not serve ads or use advertising trackers.

As a mobile app, Spajong does not use browser cookies. Our analytics and crash-reporting providers use software identifiers (such as a pseudonymous app-instance identifier) to measure usage and diagnose problems. We do not permit third parties to display advertising in the App, we do not tailor advertising to you, and we do not share your information for targeted advertising.

Google Analytics. We use Google Analytics for Firebase to understand how the App is used. For more information on Google’s privacy practices, see Google’s Privacy & Terms page.

6. Do we offer AI-based products?

In short: not in the current version. We may add AI/ML-based features in the future and will update this policy before doing so.

We may introduce AI/ML-based features in a future App update. If we do, we will update this Privacy Policy to describe how those features handle your information before they become available.

7. How long do we keep your information?

We keep your account and associated data for as long as your account exists. If you ask us to delete your account, we will delete your account data. Pseudonymous analytics and crash data are retained according to the standard retention periods of our analytics and crash-reporting providers.

8. How do we keep your information safe?

We use reasonable organizational and technical measures to protect your information, including row-level security that restricts each account to its own records. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. What are your privacy rights?

Depending on where you live, you may have rights regarding your personal information, such as the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict its use, and to withdraw consent. To exercise any of these, contact us at the email in Section 10.

  • Access and correction. You can view and update your display name and settings within the App.
  • Account and data deletion. To delete your account and associated data, email us at spajong.app@gmail.com and we will process your request.
  • Sound, haptics, and preferences. You can change these any time in the App’s settings.

Children’s privacy. The App is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, contact us and we will delete it.
